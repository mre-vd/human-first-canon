# Code of Conduct: Engineering Ethics

Our Code of Conduct is built on the principles of **Operational Stability** and **Cognitive Load Management**. We strive to create an environment where technology serves human intelligence, and where every contributor follows structured discipline.

## Our Standards

1.  **Respect the Cognitive Resource:** We understand that attention is finite. We communicate clearly, concisely, and only when necessary to reduce noise for others.
2.  **Intellectual Integrity:** We value honesty. Admitting a lack of knowledge is a sign of strength and a prerequisite for growth. We reject ego-driven certainty.
3.  **Inheritance Responsibility:** We treat every line of code as a gift to our future selves and colleagues. We provide feedback that is constructive, objective, and aimed at the work, not the person.
4.  **Agnostic Thinking:** We avoid narrow, tool-locked mindsets. We encourage thinking in principles that outlast specific technologies.
5.  **Operational Discipline:** We foster an environment where everyone is expected to follow the established canons, perform rigorous validation, and prioritize system stability.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- **Information Overloading:** Intentionally creating noise or "overheating" the cognitive resource of others.
- **Dismissive Ego:** Shutting down discussions based on authority or "obviousness" rather than technical rationale.
- **Toxicity and Harassment:** Any form of personal attack, exclusionary language, or unprofessional conduct.
- **Negligence:** Intentionally disregarding established canons or bypassing mandatory validation loops.

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported to the project maintainers. All reports will be reviewed and investigated to ensure the integrity of the engineering culture.

---

_This Code of Conduct is designed to protect the cognitive resource and professional growth of our community._
