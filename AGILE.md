# AGILE.md — Agile Delivery & Scrum Standards

Agile delivery is a sovereign domain — the flow by which analyzed work (`ANALYSIS.md`) becomes shipped value, in small, inspected steps. Read this for Scrum/Kanban roles, events, artifacts, and flow. Process serves the delivery of working software — never the reverse.

## Process Serves Delivery

Ceremony exists only to help teams deliver and learn together. A ritual that stops earning its place is waste to drop. Agile is the mindset; Scrum and Kanban are tools — pick the lightest that fits the work, and never add ceremony the team does not need.

## Roles

- **Product Owner:** owns the *what* and the order of value — the single voice for priority; orders the backlog and accepts the increment.
- **Developers:** own the *how* and the estimate; self-organizing, cross-functional, accountable for the Definition of Done.
- **Scrum Master:** clears impediments, protects focus, keeps the process honest. Authority over the process, never over the people; the team's decisions stay the team's.

## Events

- **Sprint:** a short, fixed cadence producing a usable increment. Scope is set at planning; the goal is protected from mid-sprint churn.
- **Planning:** the team *pulls* what it can finish to Done against a clear Sprint Goal — pull, never push.
- **Daily:** a brief re-sync toward the goal and surfacing of blockers — for the team, not a status report to a manager.
- **Review:** show the working increment to stakeholders and gather feedback.
- **Retrospective:** inspect the *process*, blameless, and commit to one or two real improvements that actually get done.

## Artifacts & Definition of Done

- **Product backlog:** the single ordered list of what could be done; refined continuously, with only the top made ready.
- **Sprint backlog:** the Sprint Goal plus the slice pulled to reach it; owned by the team.
- **Increment:** working, releasable software each sprint.
- **Definition of Ready:** a story enters the Sprint backlog only when its value is named, its acceptance criteria are written and agreed with the stakeholder, and blocking dependencies are surfaced and cleared — or explicitly accepted as Sprint risk. Ready gates entry; Done gates exit.
- **Definition of Done:** an explicit, shared checklist of what "complete" means — tested, reviewed, integrated, documented; nothing is Done until it meets it (*Completeness of Execution*, `CLAUDE.md`).
- **WIP limits & finishing:** limit work in progress; a thing half-done delivers nothing. Optimize for finishing, not starting.

## Estimation & Pace

- **Relative, not false-precise:** estimate by relative size (story points), not borrowed-certainty hours; the spread is information, not a promise.
- **Velocity is a forecasting tool, not a target:** it helps the team forecast; it is never a metric to push with or to compare people by. Pressuring velocity only corrupts the estimate. The team's own signals of strain — fatigue, blocked paths, growing debt — deserve equal weight.
- **Sustainable pace:** a cadence the team can hold indefinitely; burnout is waste, not virtue.
